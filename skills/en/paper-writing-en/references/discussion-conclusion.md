# Discussion and Conclusion Guide

## Discussion

The Discussion interprets results, connects them to the literature, and identifies limitations.

Include:

- What the results mean for each research question.
- Why the findings may have occurred.
- How they compare with prior work.
- What the limits of the study are.
- What is practically or theoretically useful.

## Conclusion

The Conclusion should be shorter and more direct. It should not introduce new evidence.

Include:

- Problem reminder.
- Method/artifact reminder.
- Main findings.
- Contribution.
- Limitations.
- Future work.

## Avoid

- Generic "future work includes improving the method".
- Repeating the abstract.
- Introducing new citations.
- Making claims broader than the empirical setting.
