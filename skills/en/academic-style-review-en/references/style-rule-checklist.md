# Style Rule Checklist

Use this reference for a second-pass English academic prose audit. The goal is clarity and precision, not detector evasion.

## High-priority rules

| Rule | Check | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Reader state | Does the reader know the term, acronym, dataset, method, or context before it is used? | Define or introduce before use |
| Concrete language | Are `factors`, `aspects`, `issues`, `various metrics`, or `several considerations` hiding specifics? | Name the specific mechanism, metric, dataset, or condition |
| Needless words | Does the sentence use filler such as `it is important to note`, `in order to`, `due to the fact that`? | Delete or replace with direct wording |
| Cliches | Does the prose use `paves the way`, `paradigm shift`, `unlock the potential`, `groundbreaking`? | Replace with a concrete result or delete |
| Jargon inflation | Does `leverage`, `utilize`, `facilitate`, or `operationalize` add meaning? | Use simpler wording unless the term is field-standard |
| Claim strength | Do verbs such as `proves`, `demonstrates`, or `establishes` exceed the evidence? | Weaken to `suggests`, `indicates`, or `is consistent with` |
| Terminology drift | Does one concept get multiple names to avoid repetition? | Choose one term and repeat it |
| Paragraph rhythm | Are paragraphs mechanically uniform or ending with repetitive summary sentences? | Vary structure only where it improves clarity |

## Preserve-structure invariants

When revising:

- Preserve headings, citation keys, equations, tables, code fences, links, and LaTeX commands.
- Do not add facts, metrics, sources, or claims.
- Do not rewrite paragraphs that are already clear and precise.
- Do not remove uncertainty, limitations, or scope conditions.

## Mechanical sweep

Flag these for human judgment:

- Excessive em dashes.
- Multiple semicolons in short passages.
- Consecutive paragraphs with the same list-like structure.
- Repeated transition openers: `Moreover`, `Furthermore`, `Additionally`.
- Redundant hedges: `may potentially`, `could possibly`.

## Output expectation

Report what changed and why. If a claim needs evidence, mark it as unresolved rather than inventing support.
