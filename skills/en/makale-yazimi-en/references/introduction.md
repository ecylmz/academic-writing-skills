# Introduction Writing Guide

The Introduction should build a path from task to gap to contribution.

## Recommended logic

1. Define the task and why it matters.
2. Establish the target performance, validity, scalability, or knowledge problem.
3. Explain what prior work does and where it remains insufficient.
4. Identify the technical or empirical reason behind the gap.
5. Present the proposed contribution.
6. Explain why the contribution addresses the gap.
7. Summarize results and contributions.

## Paragraph roles

- Context paragraph
- Problem paragraph
- Prior work limitation paragraph
- Gap and research question paragraph
- Method/contribution paragraph
- Results/contribution summary paragraph

## Contribution paragraph checklist

- What is new?
- What problem does it address?
- Why should it work?
- What evidence supports it?
- What is the scope?

## Avoid

- Starting with broad clichés about technology changing the world.
- Listing prior work without leading to a gap.
- Presenting the paper as a small patch over a naive baseline.
- Making claims in the Introduction that are not supported later.
